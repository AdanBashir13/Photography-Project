ICONIC SPORTS PHOTOS

MUHAMMAD ALI

Ali knocks out Cleveland 'Big Cat' Williams in three rounds at the Houston


Astrodome to defend his heavyweight title in November 1966. The bout drew a record indoor crowd of 35,460.


Many experts and boxers, including Mike Tyson, regard Ali's performance in this fight to be the finest of his boxing career.

ELIUD KIPCHOGE

Eliud Kipchoge proved No Human Is Limited when he became the first human in history to run a sub-two-hour marathon in the city of Vienna, Austria this day.


Kipchoge ran the 42.195KM distance in a remarkable time of 1:59:40:2.


He became the first ever person to run a marathon in under two hours

SERENA WILLIAMS

Serena Williams turned professional in 1995 at age 14 and it didn't take her long to claim her maiden Grand Slam.


At the 1999 US Open, the 17-year-old starlet produced an astonishing run to lift the title. She beat world No. 4 Monica Seles in the quarterfinals,


second seed Lindsey Davenport in the semifinals, and lastly world No. 1 Martina Hingis in the final.

LIONEL MESSI

The King of the Camp Nou celebrating an historic achievement with the watching fans.


When Barca fell to a shock 4-0 defeat in Paris, no one gave them a prayer in the return leg. However an inspired performance saw them come back from the dead


and win the match. Messi and Barcelona went on to carry the Champions League trophy.

MICHEAL JORDAN

Micheal Jordan was coming off five championships in seven years and was nearing the last year of his contract with the Chicago Bulls.


In a season dubbed 'The Last Dance' Jordan willed his Bulls to a third consecutive NBA championship and sixth in total.


There are so many iconic photos of Michael Jordan, but 'the shot' reigns supreme above them all.

BIOGRAPHY

SportsSnap isn't just a photography website; it's a portal to the heart-pounding, adrenaline-fueled world of sports.
Founded in 2015 by avid sports enthusiast and skilled photographer, Alex Thompson, SportsSnap quickly established itself as the go-to destination for capturing the most iconic moments in sports history. Driven by a passion for both sports and photography,
Alex Thompson envisioned SportsSnap as a platform to immortalize the moments that define sporting greatness. Armed with his camera and unwavering dedication, he embarked on a journey to capture the raw emotion, sheer athleticism, and unparalleled drama that unfold on the field, court, and track.
